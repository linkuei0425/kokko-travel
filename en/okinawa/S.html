🌺 Okinawa & Islands Deep Dive

Okinawa Main 5 Days • Kerama • Miyako

Day 1: Arrival & Kokusai Dori
Morning

Arrive at Naha Airport, take the Yui Rail to easily head to your city hotel and drop off your luggage. If time permits, enjoy a simple brunch around the airport or station.

Transport: Direct connection from Naha Airport Domestic Terminal 2F to "Naha Airport Station". Take the Yui Rail to "Prefectural Office Station" or "Makishi Station" for Kokusai Dori.
Okinawa Monorail 1-Day Pass Super Value Okinawa Fun Pass, Click Here!
Afternoon

Head to "Kokusai Dori", the bustling heart of Naha. Enjoy some Yukisio (Snow Salt) ice cream and wander through Heiwa Dori to hunt for unique souvenirs.

Evening

Dine around Kokusai Dori with Okinawa specialties like Agu Pork Shabu-Shabu or Ishigaki Beef Yakiniku. Afterwards, grab an Orion draft beer at the Yatai Mura to experience local nightlife.

Transport: Kokusai Dori Yatai Mura is located in the middle of Kokusai Dori, about a 5-minute walk from Makishi Station.
Day 2: Whale Watching & American Village Sunset
Morning

Head to Tomari Port in Naha for an awe-inspiring "Whale Watching Tour". Getting up close to wild humpback whales leaping in the sea is a must-do Okinawa experience.

Transport: About a 10-minute walk from Yui Rail "Miebashi Station" to Tomari Port. Advance booking for whale watching is usually required.
Okinawa Whale Watching Tour
Afternoon

Travel to the central area's "American Village". Stroll through the exotic American-style streets, hunt for vintage clothing and goods, and catch a beautiful sunset at Sunset Beach.

Transport: Take a local bus (No. 28, 29, or 120) from Naha Bus Terminal to "Kuwae" station, or take a direct airport limousine bus.
Evening

Enjoy a burger or steak dinner at an ocean-view restaurant in American Village. At night, the illuminated village looks like a magical world, perfect for taking photos.

Day 3: Churaumi Aquarium & Shuri Castle
Morning

Depart for the "Churaumi Aquarium" at Ocean Expo Park in northern Okinawa. Marvel at the whale sharks and manta rays in the massive "Kuroshio Sea" tank, and catch the free dolphin show.

Transport: Take the "Yanbaru Express Bus" directly to the aquarium, or join a half/full-day northern tour that includes transfers.
Churaumi Aquarium Ticket, Click Here!
Afternoon

Return to Naha and explore the "Shuri Castle Park", symbol of the Ryukyu Kingdom's glory (main hall under reconstruction but surroundings are open). Then, stroll down the ancient "Kinjo-cho Stone-Paved Road".

Transport: Take the Yui Rail to "Shuri Station", about a 15-minute walk to Shureimon Gate.
Evening

Head to the "Omoromachi" (Shintoshin) shopping district. Enjoy popular conveyor-belt sushi or ramen, then walk to the duty-free shop or a large supermarket (like Ameku Ryubo Rakuichi) for souvenirs.

Day 4: Naminoue Shrine & Senagajima Sunset
Morning

Visit "Naminoue Shrine", the chief of the Eight Ryukyu Shrines, and wade in the water at the adjacent Naminoue Beach, the only beach within Naha city.

Transport: Take the Yui Rail to "Asahibashi Station", about a 15-minute walk to the shrine.
Afternoon

Treasure hunt at the affordable "Ashibinaa Outlet", then head to "Senagajima Umikaji Terrace", often called the Little Greece of Okinawa. Find an ocean-view cafe among the white terraced buildings, enjoy a Happy Pancake, and wait for the sunset.

Transport: Take a bus from Naha Airport or Yui Rail "Akamine Station" to the Outlet, then transfer to the "Senagajima Shuttle Bus (TK02)".
Evening

After watching the planes take off and the beautiful sunset, have dinner at Senagajima, or return to traditional izakayas around "Asahibashi Station" for Okinawan home cooking and Awamori.

Day 5: Traditional Market & Tsuboya Pottery
Morning

Dive into the local kitchen, "Makishi Public Market". Pick fresh seafood on the first floor and have the restaurants on the second floor cook it for a hearty brunch.

Transport: Turn into "Ichiba Hondori" from the middle of Kokusai Dori, a 3-5 minute walk.
Afternoon

Walk to "Tsuboya Yachimun Pottery Street". Admire traditional Okinawan pottery (including various Shisa lions) on the historic stone-paved street, and relax at an old house cafe.

Transport: About a 10-minute walk from Makishi Public Market or Kokusai Dori to Tsuboya Dori.
Evening

Make a final souvenir dash at "Don Quijote" on Kokusai Dori, then take the Yui Rail to Naha Airport, heading home with wonderful memories.

The Kerama Islands are very close to Naha, famous for their world-class clear "Kerama Blue" waters and rich ecology.
🐢 Kerama Tokashiki Island Snorkeling & Sea Turtles
Transport & Island Experience

Depart by high-speed boat from Naha's Tomari Port. Arrive at Tokashiki Island's "Aharen Beach" to enjoy stunning white sands and "Kerama Blue" water. Perfect for snorkeling with a high chance of swimming with wild sea turtles.

Transport: Head to "Tomari Port" in Naha, take a high-speed boat for about 35-50 minutes. Peak season tickets sell out fast, so book online in advance.

Miyako Island boasts Japan's most beautiful beaches and three free grand bridges, making it the ultimate Okinawan resort destination.
Day 1: Arrival, Grand Bridges & Sunset
Arrival & Prep

Arrive at Miyako Airport (MMY) or Shimojishima Airport (SHI).

Rent a Car, Click Here
Afternoon: Irabu Bridge & Makiyama Observatory

Drive across Japan's longest free bridge, "Irabu Bridge", feeling surrounded by gradient blue waters.

Head to Makiyama Observatory to overlook the entire Irabu Bridge from above.

Evening: Sunayama Beach

Admire Miyako Island's famous arch-shaped rock and pure white sand, a perfect spot for sunset photos.

Dinner: Hirara City Area

Find an Okinawan-style izakaya in the city. Taste Miyako Beef Yakiniku, sea grapes, and Okinawan bitter melon stir-fry.

Day 2: Swimming with Turtles & Starry Night
Morning: Sea Turtle Snorkeling 🐢

Sea turtle snorkeling (usually at Yabiji or Aragusuku Beach). Led by an instructor, you have a high chance to see wild sea turtles and colorful coral reefs.

Afternoon: Kurima Bridge & Kurima Island

Cross Kurima Bridge to the cute Kurima Island.

Have a Miyako mango smoothie at an aesthetic cafe (like AOSORA Parlor) or overlook Yonaha Maehama Beach from Ryugujo Observatory.

Night: Starry Night & Jungle Tour ✨

Miyako Island has very low light pollution. Explore the jungle under the stars at night for unforgettable memories.

Day 3: Secret Cave & Spectacular East Coast
Morning/Afternoon: SUP x Pumpkin Cave & Canoe 🎃

Join the super popular limestone cave adventure! Accessible only at low tide, this cave features a giant pumpkin-shaped stalactite. Explore by canoe or SUP for a unique natural wonder.

Day 4: Top Eastern Beach & Souvenir Shopping
Morning: Yonaha Maehama Beach

Known as the "Best Beach in the East", featuring a 7-kilometer fine white sand beach. Seize the last moments to play in the waves, take photos, or simply bask in the Miyako Island sun.

Late Morning: Souvenir Shopping

Head to the local market (Atarasu Market / Don Quijote) for Snow Salt cookies, brown sugar, and Miyako-exclusive snacks. Don't miss the cheap and sweet local mangoes in summer.
