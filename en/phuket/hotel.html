Phuket Accommodation FAQ

Is it convenient to switch hotels frequently in Phuket?
Phuket is roughly the size of Singapore, and travel times between beaches can be long. For a 5-day trip, we strongly recommend booking a maximum of 1 or 2 hotels (e.g., 2 nights in Patong for convenience + 2 nights in a luxury villa) to avoid wasting time packing and in transit. Use apps like Bolt or Grab for seamless point-to-point travel.
Which areas are best for families with children or elderly travelers?
Many luxury villas in Phuket (like Kata Rocks) are built on cliffs with steep stairs. For elderly travelers, look for resorts on flat ground with elevators and private beaches (like Marriott Merlin Beach). For kids, Khao Lak offers incredible resorts with massive water parks and extensive kids' clubs (like The Sands Khao Lak), ensuring a stress-free holiday for parents.
How far in advance should I book? Is the rainy season worth it?
During the peak dry season (Nov-Apr), book popular oceanview hotels 3-6 months in advance. The rainy season (May-Oct) brings rough seas making it unsafe for swimming, but it's the perfect time for a 'staycation'. Ultra-luxury villas (like Rosewood or Banyan Tree) often drop their prices significantly, offering incredible value to relax indoors, enjoy the spa, and experience 5-star dining.
