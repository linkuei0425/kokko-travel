Phuket Shopping FAQ

Can I haggle at Phuket night markets? What are the tips?
Yes! Haggling is absolutely expected at traditional markets and night markets (like Patong OTOP or Naka Weekend Market) when buying clothes, elephant pants, or local crafts. A good rule of thumb is to start by offering about 30% off their initial price and keep a friendly smile. However, do not haggle in malls (like Jungceylon), pharmacies, or Big C supermarkets where prices are fixed.
How do I claim a VAT Refund for tourists in Thailand?
Shop at stores displaying the "VAT REFUND FOR TOURISTS" sign (like Central Malls or Big C). Spend at least 2,000 THB in a single store on the same day, and ask the staff for a PP10 refund form (you must have your passport). At Phuket International Airport, get your goods and forms inspected by Customs BEFORE check-in. After passing security, collect your cash refund at the VAT Refund counter inside the departure lounge.
Where is the safest place to buy authentic Thai cosmetics and herbal balms?
To ensure authenticity and quality for items applied to your skin or consumed (like Tiger Balm, Bird's Nest drinks, or Mistine cosmetics), always buy from large chain pharmacies like Watsons or Boots, or at Big C supermarkets. Avoid buying abnormally cheap health products from random street stalls to prevent purchasing counterfeits.
