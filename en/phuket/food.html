Frequently Asked Questions (Phuket Dining FAQ)

Where can I find the best and most affordable seafood in Phuket?
For fresh, budget-friendly seafood where you can pick your own live catch, head to Banzaan Fresh Market in Patong (buy downstairs, cook upstairs) or Rawai Seafood Market in the south. If you prefer a luxurious, waterfront dining experience with top-tier service, Kan Eang @ Pier at Chalong Bay is the ultimate choice.
Are there any Michelin-recommended restaurants in Phuket Old Town?
Absolutely! Phuket Old Town is recognized as a UNESCO City of Gastronomy. We strongly recommend Tu Kab Khao and One Chun. Both are situated in historic Sino-Portuguese buildings and hold the prestigious Michelin Bib Gourmand award. Don't leave without trying their signature Phuket braised pork belly (Moo Hong) and crab meat yellow curry.
What are the best night markets for street food in Phuket?
The Phuket Weekend Market (Naka Market) is the largest on the island and offers the widest variety of local Thai snacks, from grilled meats to tropical fruit smoothies (open weekends only). For weekday cravings, check out the outdoor food stalls at Banzaan Night Market in Patong (perfect for Banana Roti) or the Kata Night Market.
