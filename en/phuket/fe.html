Frequently Asked Questions (Phuket FAQ)

Is it easy to get around Phuket without renting a car?
Yes, getting around is convenient. While renting a scooter is popular, it requires an international license and caution. For safe, hassle-free travel, we highly recommend using ride-hailing apps like Grab or Bolt, which offer transparent pricing. For short distances along the beach towns, traditional Tuk-tuks are everywhere (remember to negotiate the fare first!). You can also utilize the affordable Phuket Smart Bus that travels down the west coast.
How many days do I need for a complete Phuket itinerary?
To truly experience the essence of Phuket without rushing, a 5 to 7-day itinerary is ideal. This allows you to dedicate 2 days to enjoying the west coast beaches and water activities, 1 day for cultural sightseeing (Phuket Old Town, Big Buddha, weekend night markets), and 2 full days for premier island-hopping tours like Phang Nga Bay (James Bond Island) and the Phi Phi Islands.
Are the Similan Islands open year-round for diving?
No, they are strictly seasonal. To facilitate ecological recovery and ensure tourist safety during the monsoon season, the Similan Islands National Park is officially open only from mid-October to mid-May every year. If you plan to visit Phuket during the summer months (rainy season), we recommend alternative day trips to year-round destinations like Coral Island or Racha Island.
