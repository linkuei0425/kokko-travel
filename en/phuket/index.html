Local Transport Guide

(1) Ride Apps (Grab / Bolt / InDrive)

  • Pros: Highly recommended! Transparent pricing helps you avoid tourist scams.
  • Pricing: Bolt is often cheaper than Grab. Download both to compare prices.
  • Note: Traffic near Patong can be heavy during peak hours.

(2) Tuk-Tuks / Songthaews

  • Feature: Phuket's Tuk-Tuks are usually converted small red trucks with loud music.
  • Fares: Prices are negotiated. Trips between beaches often start at 200-300 THB.
  • Rule: Always agree on the total price with the driver before getting in.

(3) Scooter / Car Rental

  • Pros: Maximum flexibility for exploring hidden beaches and viewpoints.
  • Cost: Scooters are around 250-400 THB per day.
  • Caution: An International Driving Permit is required. Thailand uses left-hand traffic with hilly, winding roads.

(4) Phuket Smart Bus

  • Route: Runs along the west coast from the Airport down to Rawai Beach.
  • Cost: Flat rate of 100 THB. Pay with exact cash or digital wallet.
  • Pros: Best option for budget travelers. It stops at all major tourist beaches.

Top Attractions Guide

(1) The Top 3 Beaches

  • Patong Beach: The epicenter of nightlife, Bangla Road, and shopping malls.
  • Karon Beach: The longest beach with plenty of water sports. Less chaotic than Patong.
  • Kata Beach: Calmer waves, perfect for beginner surfers and family vacations.

(2) Culture & Heritage

  • Old Phuket Town: Famous for Sino-Portuguese architecture, street art, and hipster cafes.
  • Big Buddha: A 45-meter tall white marble statue offering panoramic island views.
  • Wat Chalong: The largest, most revered, and most ornate Buddhist temple in Phuket.

(3) Dream Island Hopping

  • Similan Islands: World-class diving with crystal-clear water (Open Nov-Apr only).
  • Phi Phi Islands: Featuring the iconic Maya Bay from the movie "The Beach".
  • Phang Nga Bay: Sail through spectacular limestone karsts and visit James Bond Island.

Phuket Airport (HKT) Transit

(1) App Rides & Transfers

  • Private Transfer: Most convenient! Book via Klook in advance; a driver will wait with a sign.
  • Grab / Bolt: Request at designated airport zones. A ride to Patong costs ~800-1000 THB.

(2) Smart Bus

  • Pros: The ultimate choice for budget travelers! Flat rate of 100 THB.
  • Route: Departs from the airport and runs down the west coast (Kamala, Patong, Karon, Kata).
  • Frequency: Departs roughly every hour.

(3) Shared Minibus

  • Pros: Medium price range (~180-200 THB) and drops you directly at your hotel.
  • Cons: Departs only when full. They often stop at travel agencies mid-way to upsell tours (you can decline).
