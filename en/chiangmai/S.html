5 Days in Chiang Mai: The Perfect Travel Guide

Discover the top things to do in Chiang Mai. Our optimized itinerary perfectly balances ancient temples, cultural night markets, ethical elephant sanctuaries, and local culinary experiences for an unforgettable Northern Thailand vacation.

Day 1: Arrival & Old City Cultural Charm

Day 1
Daytime

Arrive at Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX) and transfer to your hotel. Begin your Northern Thailand journey by strolling through the historic Old City. Visit the iconic Tha Phae Gate and explore the majestic ruins of Wat Chedi Luang.

DIY Transport Guide: Airport Taxi (~150 THB) or Grab/Bolt to the city center. The Old City is highly walkable; alternatively, hail a iconic Red Songthaew (30 THB/ride) for local transit.
Evening

Head to the trendy Nimman Road area. Wander around One Nimman to experience the modern, artistic, and hipster vibe of Northern Thailand's cafe culture.

DIY Transport Guide: Take a Grab, Bolt, or Tuk-Tuk from the Old City to the Nimman area (approx. 80-120 THB).
Night

Immerse yourself in local street food. On weekends, the Chiang Mai Weekend Walking Streets (Saturday at South Gate, Sunday at Tha Phae Gate) are absolute must-visits. On weekdays, the Chiang Mai Night Bazaar offers incredible Northern Thai sausages and crispy banana roti.

DIY Transport Guide: Walk or grab a Red Songthaew (30 THB) depending on your hotel location.

Day 2: Doi Suthep Temple & Traditional Khantoke

Day 2
Morning

Travel up the mountain to visit the sacred Wat Phra That Doi Suthep, one of Northern Thailand's most important temples, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the entire Chiang Mai valley.

DIY Transport Guide: Catch a Red Songthaew from Chiang Mai University or North Gate (~60-80 THB/way, waits to fill up). Alternatively, charter a private red truck for ~600 THB round trip.
Afternoon

Relax after the mountain descent at one of Chiang Mai's famous jungle-style cafes (such as Chom Cafe and Restaurant or No.39 Cafe) for premium Thai coffee and Instagram-worthy photos.

DIY Transport Guide: Use Grab or Bolt for convenient point-to-point travel to specific cafes.
Night

Experience a culturally rich Khantoke Dinner. Savor authentic Khao Soi (Northern Thai coconut curry noodles) while enjoying classical Lanna Thai dance performances.

Reserve Old Chiang Mai Cultural Center Khantoke
DIY Transport Guide: Grab or Bolt is the easiest and safest way to reach the cultural center at night.

Day 3: Ethical Elephant Sanctuary & Thai Spa

Day 3
Daytime

Join an ethical Elephant Sanctuary tour (100% no riding). Change into traditional mahout clothing, hand-make vitamin rice balls to feed the gentle giants, and enjoy a memorable mud bath with them in the jungle.

Book Bestselling Elephant Jungle Sanctuary Tour
DIY Transport Guide: Almost all ethical sanctuary tours include convenient free round-trip hotel transfers.
Night

Head to the Old City's North Gate (Chang Phuak Gate) to savor the legendary Michelin-recommended "Cowboy Hat Lady Pork Leg Rice" (Khao Kha Moo). Conclude your day with an authentic and deeply relaxing traditional Thai massage.

Reserve Fah Lanna Premium Spa Experience
DIY Transport Guide: Red Songthaew (30 THB) or Grab to the North Gate for street food and spa.

Day 4: Thai Cooking Class & Creative Art Village

Day 4
Morning

Enroll in an authentic Thai Cooking Class. Start with a local market tour to source fresh ingredients, then learn to cook classic dishes like Tom Yum Goong, Green Curry, and sweet Mango Sticky Rice. Enjoy your masterpiece for lunch!

Book Fun Thai Cooking Class (Beginner Friendly)
DIY Transport Guide: Cooking classes typically offer free hotel pickup and drop-off.
Afternoon

Explore Baan Kang Wat, a vibrant, green-filled creative artisan village. Experience pottery making, watercolor painting, or simply relax with artisanal coffee at an independent craft bookstore.

DIY Transport Guide: Located slightly out of the main town. Highly recommend taking a Grab or Bolt (~100-150 THB).
Night

Take a scenic stroll along the Mae Ping River. Choose a popular riverside restaurant like The Good View or Riverside Bar for a romantic dinner accompanied by superb live band performances.

DIY Transport Guide: Grab/Bolt is the optimal choice for evening riverside travel.

Day 5: Local Markets, Souvenirs & Departure

Day 5
Morning

Dive into the bustling Warorot Market (Kad Luang), the largest traditional indoor market in Chiang Mai. Stock up on authentic local souvenirs: crispy pork rinds (Kab Moo), green curry paste, northern spices, and premium dried mangoes.

DIY Transport Guide: Take a Red Songthaew (30 THB) or easily walk if you are staying near Tha Phae Gate.
Afternoon

Complete your last-minute shopping at the modern Maya Lifestyle Shopping Center in Nimman. Afterward, transfer to the airport or prepare for your onward journey to neighboring gems like Chiang Rai or Pai.

DIY Transport Guide: Grab/Bolt (~150-200 THB) from Maya Mall to CNX Airport. Plan to depart 2.5 hours prior to your flight due to city traffic.

Chiang Rai Travel Guide

A Must-Do Day Trip: Located about a 3-hour drive north of Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai is globally famous for its extreme contemporary art temples. Booking a guided 1-day tour is highly recommended for convenience.

🏛️ Iconic Chiang Rai Temples 1-Day Tour

Morning

Depart from Chiang Mai with a refreshing stop at the Mae Khachan Hot Springs. Upon arriving in Chiang Rai, experience the awe-inspiring White Temple (Wat Rong Khun), a pristine contemporary masterpiece depicting heaven and rebirth.

Book White, Blue & Black Temple Tour
DIY Transport Guide: Take the VIP "Green Bus" from Chiang Mai Arcade Bus Terminal 3 (~3.5h, ~200-300 THB). Booking via their App in advance is mandatory.
Afternoon

Continue your visual journey at the striking Blue Temple (Wat Rong Suea Ten) with its vibrant sapphire hues. Following that, delve into the dark, mysterious, and fascinating architecture of the Baan Dam Museum (Black House).

DIY Transport Guide: Chiang Rai attractions are spread far apart. If doing DIY, renting a scooter or relying heavily on Grab/Bolt is required (Hence, day tours are much easier).
Evening

Opt for a visit to the Long Neck Karen Village to discover local ethnic minority heritage, before boarding your bus or van back to your Chiang Mai accommodation.

DIY Transport Guide: Return via the Green Bus, or relax in your air-conditioned guided tour van.

Doi Inthanon Hiking Guide

The Roof of Thailand: Located a 2-hour drive from Chiang Mai. As Thailand's highest peak, it offers refreshing cool weather year-round, stunning waterfalls, and world-class nature trails.

🏔️ Doi Inthanon National Park Exploration

Morning

Journey into Doi Inthanon National Park. Snap a photo at the highest point monument in Thailand. Marvel at the magnificent King and Queen Twin Royal Pagodas, encircled by manicured botanical gardens and surreal seas of clouds.

Book Premium Doi Inthanon Day Tour
DIY Transport Guide: Public transit is non-existent here. You must charter a private Red Songthaew/Taxi for the full day (~2,500-3,500 THB) or join a guided tour.
Afternoon

Visit an authentic Karen Hill Tribe village and tour the Royal Agricultural Station to taste exquisite locally grown high-altitude Arabica coffee. On your descent, be mesmerized by the thunderous power of Wachirathan Waterfall.

DIY Transport Guide: Self-driving (car or high-CC scooter) is possible but strictly recommended only for highly experienced drivers due to treacherous, steep, and winding mountain passes.

Pai Adventure & Backpacking Guide

The Bohemian Mountain Town: Reached via a famous 762-curve road trip from Chiang Mai (approx. 3 hours). Pai is Thailand's ultimate hippie paradise filled with hot springs, canyons, and extreme outdoor sports.

🛵 Pai Extreme Adventure & Nature Tour

Morning

Gear up for an adrenaline-pumping morning. Navigate muddy jungle trails on a powerful ATV, soar through the forest canopy via zipline, and conquer the river rapids with white water rafting.

DIY Transport Guide: Chiang Mai to Pai: Book a Minivan (Prempracha Transport) from Chiang Mai Arcade Bus Terminal 2 (~3h, ~150 THB). Take motion sickness pills for the 762 curves!
Afternoon

Discover the incredible Bua Tong Sticky Waterfall. Thanks to mineral deposits, the limestone rocks provide natural grip, allowing you to literally climb up the cascading waterfall like Spiderman!

Book Sticky Waterfall & ATV Adventure
DIY Transport Guide: Inside Pai, public transport is virtually non-existent. Renting a scooter (~150-250 THB/day) is the absolute best way to explore Pai Canyon and surrounding sights.
