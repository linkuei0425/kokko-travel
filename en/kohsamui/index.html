Island Transport Guide

(1) Songthaew (Red Truck)

  • Feature: The most common and affordable transport. Flag them down, ring the bell to stop, and pay the driver directly.
  • Cost: Around 50 THB within the same area, 100-150 THB across areas.
  • Note: Confirm destination and price before boarding. At night, they may operate as private taxis with negotiated rates.

(2) Scooter / Car Rental

  • Feature: Offers the highest freedom to explore the island at your own pace.
  • Cost: Scooters ~200-300 THB/day; Cars start from ~1000 THB/day.
  • Note: An International Driving Permit is required. Thailand drives on the left side of the road. Drive carefully on hilly terrains!

(3) Taxi

  • Feature: Air-conditioned and comfortable.
  • Fare: Most taxis on the island are unmetered. Prices are negotiated and usually start from 300-400 THB.
  • Tip: Use ride-hailing apps like Grab or inDrive for more transparent pricing.

(4) Chartered Car (Private Driver)

  • Feature: Ideal for families, groups, or multi-stop day trips (e.g., Big Buddha, Hin Ta Hin Yai, Waterfalls).
  • Cost: Half-day or full-day rates range from 1500-3000 THB, including the driver. The most relaxing way to travel.

Top Attractions

(1) Beaches & Natural Wonders

  • Chaweng Beach: The longest and liveliest beach with fine white sand, water sports, and bustling nightlife.
  • Lamai Beach: The second-largest beach, offering a quieter and more relaxing atmosphere.
  • Hin Ta Hin Yai: Famous "Grandpa & Grandma Rocks" at the south end of Lamai, shaped by nature and perfect for photos.

(2) Culture & Temples

  • Big Buddha (Wat Phra Yai): Koh Samui's most iconic landmark, featuring a 12-meter tall golden Buddha statue.
  • Wat Plai Laem: A colorful Thai-Chinese temple known for its massive 18-arm Guanyin statue.
  • Wat Khunaram (Mummified Monk): Showcases the mummified body of a revered monk, reflecting deep Thai Buddhist beliefs.

(3) Island Hopping & Nature

  • Ang Thong National Park: A stunning archipelago of 42 limestone islands, perfect for kayaking, snorkeling, and hiking.
  • Koh Tao & Koh Nang Yuan: Koh Tao is a diving paradise; Koh Nang Yuan is famous for its iconic Y-shaped sandbar.
  • Koh Phangan: Located nearby and world-renowned for its monthly Full Moon Party.

Airport Transport Guide

(1) Airport Minibus

  • Pros: Affordable and drops you right at your hotel's doorstep.
  • Note: Departs only when full, so there might be some waiting time.
  • Tickets: Available at the transport counters in the arrivals hall.

(2) Private Taxi / Transfer

  • Pros: Leaves immediately, offers privacy, great for families or groups.
  • Cost: ~400-500 THB to Chaweng; ~600+ THB to Lamai.

(3) Hotel Transfer

  • Pros: Hassle-free! Your driver will be waiting with a name sign at arrivals.
  • Tip: Email your hotel to book in advance. Some luxury resorts offer it for free.

Bus + Ferry Joint Ticket

To save on flights, many travelers fly into Surat Thani (URT) and take a bus + ferry to Koh Samui. It takes longer but is a great local experience!

  • Route: Arrive at URT → Bus to Donsak Pier (~1.5h) → Ferry to Koh Samui (~1.5h).
  • Tickets: Book joint tickets via AirAsia, Nok Air, or buy them at URT arrivals (from operators like Lomprayah or Seatran).
