Transport Guide

(1) Songthaew (Red Truck)

  • Feature: The most popular local transport. Flag them down, ring the bell to stop, and pay directly.
  • Cost: ~50 THB within the same area, 100-150 THB across different beaches.
  • Pro Tip: Negotiate pricing before boarding at night, as they operate like private taxis.

(2) Scooter / Car Rental

  • Feature: Ultimate freedom for exploring hidden beaches and waterfalls.
  • Cost: Scooters from 200-300 THB/day; Cars from 1000 THB/day.
  • Safety Note: An International Driving Permit is required. Thailand drives on the left.

(3) Private Taxis & Apps

  • Feature: Air-conditioned, reliable, and comfortable.
  • Fare: Taxis are mostly unmetered. Rates start around 300-400 THB.
  • Apps: Use Grab or inDrive for transparent, upfront pricing.

Top Things to Do

(1) Best Beaches

  • Chaweng Beach: The most famous, bustling beach with powdery white sand and vibrant nightlife.
  • Lamai Beach: A quieter, family-friendly alternative with great local dining options.
  • Hin Ta Hin Yai: The iconic "Grandpa & Grandma Rocks", a natural wonder near Lamai.

(2) Temples & Culture

  • Big Buddha (Wat Phra Yai): Koh Samui's iconic 12-meter golden Buddha overlooking the sea.
  • Wat Plai Laem: Features a breathtaking 18-arm statue of Guanyin, the Goddess of Mercy.
  • Mummified Monk: Wat Khunaram showcases Thailand's deep spiritual roots.

(3) Island Hopping

  • Ang Thong Marine Park: An archipelago of 42 pristine islands for snorkeling and kayaking.
  • Koh Tao & Koh Nang Yuan: World-class diving spots with stunning crystal-clear waters.
  • Koh Phangan: Home to the legendary Full Moon Party, just a short ferry ride away.
