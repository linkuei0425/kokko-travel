Changi Airport Guide (SIN)

(1) MRT

  • Route Info: The station is located between T2 and T3. Take the Green Line (CG Branch) and alight at "Tanah Merah" station. Walk to the opposite platform to transfer to the city-bound train (towards Tuas Link).
  • Payment: Use Apple Pay/Google Pay or a contactless credit card to tap in directly (SimplyGo). No need to buy tickets.
Singapore NETS FlashPay Card

(2) Ride-hailing (Grab / Gojek) & Private Transfers

  • Ride-hailing: Most convenient. Follow the signs to the dedicated Ride Hailing zones at each terminal. Fare to the city is around $20-30 SGD, taking 20-30 mins.
  • Private Transfers: Highly recommended for groups with elders, kids, or lots of luggage. Pre-book a private car.
Book Private Airport Transfer

(3) Airport Shuttle & Bus

  • City Shuttle: 24/7 shared bus service dropping off at major city hotels, ~$10 SGD per way.
  • Bus: Take Bus 36 to Orchard Road or Marina Bay. Good for travelers with less luggage and more time.

Jewel Changi Must-Visit Guide

  • Location: Located in front of T1 Arrival Hall. Walkable via link bridges from T2/T3 (5-10 mins). From T4, take the free shuttle bus first.
  • Rain Vortex: World's tallest indoor waterfall. Free light and sound show at night.
  • Early Check-in: Many airlines allow early baggage drop at Jewel (L1), so you can shop and eat hands-free (e.g., Shake Shack, Song Fa Bak Kut Teh).

Singapore Multicultural Districts

Singapore is a multi-ethnic melting pot. Step into these cultural enclaves and feel like you've traveled to another country. Great for exotic vibes and authentic food.

📍 Chinatown

A blend of traditional and modern Chinese heritage

Visit the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, try Tian Tian Chicken Rice at Maxwell Food Centre, and grab a cocktail at Ann Siang Hill at night.

MRT Blue/Purple Line "Chinatown"

📍 Little India

Colorful spices and floral garlands

Visit the Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple, shop at the 24/7 Mustafa Centre for affordable souvenirs, and enjoy authentic prata and teh tarik.

MRT Blue/Purple Line "Little India"

📍 Kampong Glam

Arabian charm and hipster alleys

Centered around the stunning Sultan Mosque. Haji Lane is full of street art, indie boutiques, and cafes, making it a youth favorite.

MRT Green/Blue Line "Bugis"
