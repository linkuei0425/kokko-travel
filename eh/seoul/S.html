Seoul 5 Days • Nami & Gapyeong • Suwon • Ski

Day 1: Arrival in Seoul & Myeongdong Exploration
Daytime

Arrive at Incheon or Gimpo International Airport and head to your hotel to drop off luggage (Myeongdong, Hongdae, or Dongdaemun recommended). In the afternoon, head to Myeongdong shopping district, where you can find the most complete Korean beauty brands, Olive Young flagship store, and rich street food.

Transport: From Incheon Airport, taking the AREX express train directly to Seoul Station is recommended, then transfer to the subway to your hotel; Myeongdong is located at Subway Line 4 "Myeongdong Station".

Evening

Enjoy authentic Myeongdong Kyoja or Sinseon Seolleongtang, then take the Namsan Cable Car up to N Seoul Tower.

Transport: About 10-15 mins walk from Myeongdong Station Exit 3 to the Namsan Cable Car boarding point.
Night

Overlook the beautiful and romantic city night view from N Seoul Tower, lock a love padlock, and feel the charming appeal of Seoul.

Day 2: Hanbok Experience & Traditional Market
Morning

Head to Gyeongbokgung Palace. Rent a beautiful traditional Hanbok nearby; wearing a Hanbok grants free admission to the palace. Experience the fun of traveling back to the Joseon Dynasty and watch the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony on the hour.

Transport: Walk directly from Gyeongbokgung Station (Line 3) Exit 5. There are many Hanbok rental shops nearby.
Book Seohwa Hanbok Experience at Gyeongbokgung, Click Here!
Afternoon

Stroll to the historically rich Bukchon Hanok Village and find the classic Eight Views of Bukchon. Then head to Samcheong-dong or Insadong to browse traditional Korean crafts and unique cafes.

Transport: About 15 mins walk from Gyeongbokgung to Bukchon Hanok Village; Insadong is close to Anguk Station (Line 3).
Night

Head to Gwangjang Market for local food! Must-eats include mung bean pancakes (Bindaetteok), Mayak Gimbap, and Michelin-recommended beef tartare (Yukhoe). Experience the most vibrant Korean market culture.

Transport: Walk directly from Jongno 5(o)-ga Station (Line 1) Exit 8.
Day 3: Seongsu-dong Hotspots & Gangnam Style
Daytime

Visit Seoul's trendiest "Brooklyn of Korea" — Seongsu-dong. It's filled with industrial-style cafes converted from factories (like Cafe Onion), the Dior Seongsu concept store, and physical stores of trending brands like Tamburins and Matin Kim.

Transport: The area around Seongsu Station (Line 2) is entirely the prime district for walking.
Afternoon

Cross the Han River to the Gangnam district and visit the Starfield Library at COEX to photograph the spectacular giant book walls. Afterward, shop around the Gangnam Underground Shopping Center or Apgujeong Rodeo to experience the refined lifestyle of Seoul's high society.

Transport: Starfield Library is located inside the COEX Mall, accessible from Samseong Station (Line 2) Exits 5 and 6.
Night

Arrive at Banpo Hangang Park in the evening. Order Korean fried chicken and beer for delivery, enjoy the stunning Moonlight Rainbow Fountain of Banpo Bridge, and experience a standard K-drama daily life.

Transport: About 15 mins walk from Express Bus Terminal Station (Lines 3, 7, 9) Exit 8-1.
Day 4: Hongdae Youth Vibe & Sinchon Nightlife
Morning

In the morning, head to Seoul's youth landmark, Hongdae. First, stroll along the Gyeongui Line Forest Trail in Yeonnam-dong nearby, which has many hidden aesthetic dessert shops and brunches.

Transport: Hongik Univ. Station (Line 2) Exit 3 leads to Yeonnam-dong; Exit 9 leads to the main Hongdae shopping district.
Afternoon

Go on a shopping spree for clothes, stationery, and sneakers on the main street of Hongdae (like MUSINSA, SHOOPEN). Prices here are very youth-friendly. On weekends, you can also enjoy street performances.

Night

For dinner, taste Hongdae's famous Korean BBQ or grilled intestines (Gopchang). After eating, head to Sinchon to experience the Pojangmacha (tented food stalls), or visit live bars and clubs in Hongdae to feel the energy of the sleepless city.

Day 5: Mart Shopping & Departure
Morning

Seize your last moments and head to Lotte Mart at Seoul Station for a massive shopping haul! Must-buys include Korean ramen, snacks, seaweed, and various souvenirs. You can process tax refunds directly inside the supermarket, which is very convenient.

Transport: Right next to Seoul Station Exit 1 (Lines 1, 4, and AREX intersection).
Afternoon

Return to your hotel to pick up your luggage, and take the AREX express train or an airport bus to Incheon/Gimpo Airport. Head home with a flight full of memories and spoils.

Gapyeong is about 1 to 1.5 hours' drive from central Seoul, known for its beautiful natural scenery and classic K-drama filming locations.
🌲 Nami Island & Gapyeong Healing 1-Day Tour
Morning

Take the ITX-Cheongchun train to Gapyeong. First, take a ferry to Nami Island. Stroll along the Metasequoia path and feel the romantic atmosphere of "Winter Sonata". The island has different beautiful scenery in all four seasons, with autumn foliage and winter snow being particularly dreamy.

Transport: Take the ITX-Cheongchun train from Yongsan Station or Cheongnyangni Station in Seoul to Gapyeong Station, then transfer to a taxi or shuttle bus to the pier.
From Seoul: Nami Island & Petite France Day Tour, Skip the Line & Enjoy!
Afternoon

Enjoy the local specialty "Chuncheon Dakgalbi" (Spicy Stir-fried Chicken) for lunch. In the afternoon, head to Petite France, full of European fairy-tale vibes, or visit the Garden of Morning Calm to admire the meticulously arranged Korean garden plants.

Transport: Take the "Gapyeong City Tour Bus"; one ticket allows unlimited rides, connecting Gapyeong Station, Nami Island, Petite France, and the Garden of Morning Calm.
Evening

If you like outdoor activities, you can also plan for the Gangchon Rail Bike. Ride along the old railway, pass through valley tunnels and the Bukhan River, enjoying excellent scenery. Return to Seoul by train afterward.

Suwon is located south of Seoul and is easily accessible by subway. It boasts spectacular World Cultural Heritage sites and a highly popular fried chicken street.
🏰 Suwon Hwaseong & Trendy Library 1-Day Tour
Morning

Upon arriving in Suwon, first visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Suwon Hwaseong Fortress. It's a well-preserved Joseon Dynasty fortress where you can walk along the fortress walls or take the Hwaseong Trolley for an easy tour.

Transport: Take a train (KTX/Mugunghwa) or Subway Line 1 from Seoul Station to Suwon Station, then transfer to a city bus for about 15 minutes to reach Hwaseong.
From Seoul: Suwon Hwaseong & Korean Folk Village Day Tour, Click Here!
Afternoon

Head to the recently viral photo spot, Suwon Starfield (Starfield Library 2.0). It is grander and more spectacular than the COEX library, serving as the latest landmark combining shopping and leisure. Alternatively, go to Haenggung-dong Cafe Street to find a unique Hanok cafe to enjoy the afternoon.

Transport: Suwon Starfield is located next to Hwaseo Station (Line 1), just one stop away from Suwon Station by subway.
Night

For dinner, you absolutely cannot miss Suwon Fried Chicken Street. The "Suwon Galbi Sauce Fried Chicken," which went viral due to the movie "Extreme Job", features a sweet and savory sauce with a crispy crust. Pair it with a beer for a perfect end to your day trip.

Transport: The fried chicken street is located near Paldalmun of Suwon Hwaseong, accessible by walking or a short bus ride. After eating, take a bus back to Suwon Station and transfer back to Seoul.

⛷️ Seoul Suburbs Fun Ski 1-Day Tour
Morning

Gather and depart for the ski resort (Vivaldi Park or Elysian Gangchon recommended). After arriving, rent ski equipment and clothing. Beginners are strongly advised to take basic ski lessons (Ski or Snowboard).

Transport: It is strongly recommended to directly book a ski day tour departing from Seoul, which includes shuttle transport, equipment rental, and coaches, saving you the hassle of transferring yourself.
Elysian Gangchon Ski Resort One Day Tour, Click Here!
Afternoon

Enjoy the fun of skiing! Take the ski lift to slopes of different gradients, or practice repeatedly in the beginner area. For non-skiers, you can take the sightseeing gondola to the summit or play with snow sleds and tubes at Snowy Land.

Evening / Night

Return the equipment and take the shuttle bus or coach back to central Seoul (usually dropping off at Hongdae or Myeongdong). Dinner is perfect for enjoying piping hot Dak-hanmari (One Whole Chicken) or Budae-jjigae (Army Stew) to warm up your body.
